Breathing Reset

Inhale for 4 seconds, exhale for 6–8. Repeat 5–10x. Focus on belly rising and falling, not chest.

Vagus Nerve Toning

Hum, gargle, or sing loudly for 1–2 minutes. Also try slow neck rotations while exhaling.

Somatic Shake

Stand and shake your arms, legs, and hips for 1–2 minutes. Let your body move freely.

Balloon Visualization

Imagine packing your worries into a backpack, tying it to a balloon, and watching it float away.

Soothing Touch

Place one hand on your heart, one on your belly. Say: “I’m safe. I’m here. I’m okay.”

Grounding Walk

Walk barefoot or slowly outside. Notice 5 things you see, hear, and feel.

⬆️ Back to top

Orienting

Turn your head slowly left and right. Let your eyes gently scan your environment.

Butterfly Hug

Cross arms over chest and tap shoulders alternately. Helps calm the nervous system.

⬆️ Back to top

Sighing Breath

Inhale through nose, long sigh out through mouth. Repeat 3–5x.

⬆️ Back to top

Progressive Relaxation

Tense and release one muscle group at a time from feet to head.

⬆️ Back to top

Eye Movement Reset

Look left, then right, repeating for 30–60 seconds while breathing slowly.

⬆️ Back to top

Mindful Sipping

Sip a warm drink slowly, focusing completely on the sensation.

⬆️ Back to top

Face Massage

Use fingers to gently massage jaw, temples, forehead, and under cheekbones.

⬆️ Back to top

Heart Focus

Close eyes and bring attention to heart. Breathe slowly while imagining warmth there.

⬆️ Back to top

Self-Compassion Phrase

Silently say: 'This is hard. I’m doing my best. I am here for me.'

⬆️ Back to top

Cold Splash

Splash cold water on your face or use a cold pack on your neck to engage vagus nerve.

⬆️ Back to top

Havening

Gently stroke arms from shoulders to elbows. Say calming phrases or count slowly.

⬆️ Back to top

Scent Reset

Smell something calming like lavender or citrus and breathe in slowly 3 times.

⬆️ Back to top

Mini Dance

Play a favorite song and move however your body wants to for 1–2 minutes.

⬆️ Back to top

Visualization: Safe Place

Close eyes and imagine a place you feel completely safe. Describe it to yourself in detail.
